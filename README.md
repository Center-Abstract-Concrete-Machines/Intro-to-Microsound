> “Beneath the level of the note lies the realm of microsound, of sound particles. Microsonic particles remained invisible for centuries. Recent technological advances let us probe and explore the beauties of this formerly unseen world. Microsonic techniques dissolve the rigid bricks of musical architecture – the notes – into a more fluid and supple medium. Sound may coalesce, evaporate, or mutate into other sounds.” - Curtis Roads, Microsound (2001)

What does a 41000th of a second sound like?

Intro  to Microsound is set of patches introducing students to the Gen~ programming environment inside of MaxMSP. Graham Wakefield, a student of Curtis Roads, created Gen~ to allow for the visually patching and coding of audio at the level of an individual sample of digital signal (i.e. the programs we will write will run more than 41000 times per  second). You can read his dissertation [here](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.5555/2519781) and an interview with him on gen~ [here](https://www.perfectcircuit.com/signal/graham-wakefield-generating-sound-organizing-time-interview).

This course serves as a technical introduction to Gen~ couched in the environment’s conceptual strengths: microsound and processual composition. It is intended for both experienced sound programmers as well as brave newcomers; both will walk about with new techniques and new ways of thinking about working with sound.

These materials intend to bring you from Gen~ fundamentals of constructing sonic and timing structures one single sample at a time to diffusing dynamic and long-form sonic textures via microsound techniques leading towards granular and pulsar synthesis. This course material was created in service of a course I taught as a collaboration between CCAM and  the Experimental Sound Studio in Chicago in Winter 2025. 

The recordings of those sessions are publically available [here](https://ccam.world/resources/intro-to-microsound-vid/). I'm currently making a more focused second pass through this material in preparation for applying Gen~ to hardware applications (e.g. OopsyDaisy, possibly RNBO).

Contained here are the examples I have built in support of the course. I may also build out include some custom operators (you may know this as abstractions or  objects in Max/MSP or functions in programming languages).
